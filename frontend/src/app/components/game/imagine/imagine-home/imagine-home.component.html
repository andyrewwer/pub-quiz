Imagine If is the game where it’s not what you think of yourself, but how others imagine you that matters.

Each round a question about one of the players will be posed and six possible answers displayed. It’s your job to imagine what that player would be, choose, or do. In this game the majority rules—all who select the most popular answer will be awarded one point, whether or not the answer agrees with the subject’s own answer about themself. If however, the subject’s own answer matches the majority, that player will be awarded two points. In case of tie, all majorities will be given points.

The first player to 15 points wins.
